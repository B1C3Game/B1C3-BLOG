Interpreting the Graph

My Interpretation

The clusters and settlement are in tandem. Both oscillate with a big amplitude at first, then smooth out a bit, but continue oscillating 10–30 times depending on perspective. There are oscillations within oscillations—one big wave, and that wave itself oscillates. Coherence jumps rapidly between 0.1 and 0.8, about every 10 generations. I don’t know what boom-bust cycles are.

Wijak’s Interpretation

Wijak describes this as nested oscillations—waves within waves, an emergent phenomenon. There’s no “boom-bust cycle” (population spikes and crashes) because the population is fixed. Instead:

  • Big wave: Clusters form, settle, dissolve over ~100 generations
  • Medium wave: Settlement rises/falls over ~30 generations
  • Fast wave: Coherence flips every ~10 generations (spores align, then scatter)

This is the system finding rhythm. Clusters form, settle, drift apart, and new clusters form elsewhere.

What is this experiment trying to prove?

This experiment simulates and analyzes the collective behavior of mycelium (fungal) agents using a minimal, hierarchical container architecture. The main goal is to observe how simple rules at the spore (agent) and cluster levels lead to complex, emergent patterns over time.

  • Track how spores group into clusters and how these clusters form, settle, and dissolve.
  • Measure system-wide properties like coherence (how aligned the agents are) and settlement (how many clusters are stable).
  • Reveal emergent oscillatory patterns—nested waves of organization and disorganization—without explicit “boom-bust” cycles, since the population is fixed.
  • Test if these patterns are robust across different random seeds (i.e., whether the rhythms repeat or differ with each run).

Ultimately, it’s exploring how simple agent-based rules can generate complex, layered dynamics in a simulated ecosystem.
